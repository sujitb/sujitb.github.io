Recap:

In the third session, we used the accelerometer sensor in the Micro:bit buttons. To summarise we:

1.    Made use of the Accelerometer to display icons (images) and also played some music.

2.    We know how the accelerometer works in terms of simple mechanics of solid ball inside a cube.

3.    We have also seen the Micro-electro-mechanical (MEMS) mechanism of the accelerometer.

4.    We have 3-accelerometer sensors to sense the direction in x-axis, y-axis and z-axis.

5.    The Micro:bit measures the accelerometer value in milli-gravity.

6.    We defined acceleration and also observed that this result in a force.

7.    We then discussed gravity and observed the effect of releasing a feather and a metal ball in free space and in the vacuum

8.    We created simple programs to observe the sensor values when it is kept flat on the table. The z-axis always shows a reading of -1024 (the maximum) due to the acceleration due to gravity).  

9.    We did understand the touch sensitive plants in the nature.

10.   We created projects related to shake including the Electronic Magic Candle.

11. We introduced the concept of a random number, variables, conditional statements (if-then) and comparison (less than, greater than and equal to).

12. Coded programs, simulated the program, saved the programs on the laptop, downloaded the program in the Micro:bit and ran the program on the Micro:bit.

13. We used accelerometer as sensors, Micro:bit program as the processing power and the screen as an actuator. All three elements sensor, brain and actuator were in the Micro:bit. 

14. Discussed the various stages of creating the complete project -- requirement, design, code, simulate, check, store, download (flash) and run.

15. In the software platform Makecode, we can press the button shake to simulate the motion or move the Micro:bit on the screen with the mouse to simulate the program.

16. Advanced students were asked to check the Javascript code and modify its contents.

 

 

What did they learn:
Technology Related

  1. Accelerometer working principle.
  2. Micro-Electro-Mechanical devices.
  3. The accelerometer can detect movement in 3-axes: x-axis, y-axis and z-axis.
  4. Interrupt to the processor (remind me in the session, if not covered)

Science Related

  1. Difference between speed and acceleration 
  2. Force due to acceleration
  3. Acceleration due to the Earth's gravitational pull.
  4. Feather and Ball experiment to show the effect of air resistance.
  5. Unit of acceleration in terms of milli-gravity (mg).

Coding Related

  1. Random number generation
  2. Create a new variable.
  3. Assign a value to the variable
  4. Conditional statement (if-then)
  5. Comparison (Greater than, less than and equal to)
  6. On shake command.

Assignments

Project A:
Electronic Dice

1.    Create a variable dice

2.    Assign the variable dice a random number between 1 to 6

3.    On shake display the dice dots depending on the value of the dice.

 

Project B:
Rock-Paper-Scissor

1.    Create a variable choice

2.    Assign the variable choice a random number between 1 to 3

3.    On shake display the icon of

a)    Rock if choice is 1.

b)    Paper if choice is 2.

c)    Scissor is choice is 3.

 

Project C:
Arrows based on Tilts

1.    Show a left arrow when the Micro:bit is tilted left.

2.    Show a right arrow when the Micro:bit is tilted right.

3.    Show a Up arrow when the Micro:bit is tilted up.

4.    Show a Down arrow when the Micro:bit is tilted down.



Project D:
Play different tunes based on how fast we move the kit (Acceleration) 

1.    Make use of 3g, 6g, 8g and free fall of the accelerometer to play various tunes.

2.    Connect to the speaker to check the sound.
